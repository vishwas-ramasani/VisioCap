Visio-Cap is a low-cost, head-worn wearable assistive device designed to enhance the mobility and safety of visually impaired individuals. The system integrates three HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensors, an HC-SR501 Passive Infrared (PIR) motion sensor, and a rain/moisture detection module, all coordinated by an Arduino Nano microcontroller. The primary purpose of the device is to detect overhead and mid-level obstacles — a critical gap left unaddressed by traditional assistive tools such as the white cane. The system continuously monitors the user's surrounding space in three directions: left, front, and right. When an obstacle enters a predefined caution zone, the corresponding vibration motor in the headband fires, providing the user with directional tactile feedback. When an object enters the danger zone, both the motor and a buzzer activate simultaneously to deliver an immediate multi-sensory warning. The PIR sensor adds an additional layer of intelligence by distinguishing between stationary objects and moving warm bodies such as pedestrians, enabling escalated and more contextually relevant alerts. A key innovation of Visio-Cap is its rain-adaptive filtering mechanism. Moisture detected on the cap's rain sensor triggers the firmware to automatically switch to a smoothed averaging mode and raise the minimum detection distance, effectively eliminating false alarms caused by water droplet interference. This ensures reliable performance in outdoor weather conditions where most similar devices fail. The entire system runs on a single 18650 lithium-ion cell, managed by a TP4056 charging module and an MT3608 boost converter. The hardware is compact, lightweight, and embedded within a standard cap form factor, allowing users to wear it throughout the day without discomfort. Experimental validation confirmed correct directional alerts across all three distance bands under normal conditions and demonstrated effective noise rejection in simulated rain environments.
